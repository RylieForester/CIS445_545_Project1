International Baccalaureate (IB) International Night (2020)

In high school, I was a part of the IB Academic Program, and one of the many events that incorporate IB students' work is International Night. IB students form pairs together and create a poster consisting of information about a country of their choosing. In addition to the poster, students would also bring in two food items that originated from their assigned country. Students who were taking a particular foreign language class tended to pick countries that spoke the language they were learning. This informational event allowed students, teachers, and anyone who attended to learn more about different countries and gave them an opportunity to try new foods that are popular in other countries.

I participated in International Night both my junior and senior year; however, in addition to participating in the event as a senior, I, along with another student, were in charge of coordinating the entire event. This involved us meeting up often with each other and with our teacher advisor to discuss certain aspects of the event. For example, there was a raffle wherein attendees of the event had a chance to win a variety of different ethnic foods. My partner and I had to set up the particulars pertaining to the raffle, and we made several trips to different ethnic markets to buy food as the raffle prizes. Another aspect of the event was deciding how to set up the large area we had to work within, so that it was not only organized with everyone's work presented clearly, but we also needed to take into consideration which countries to group together and which booths needed outlets for their food.

Though it was a lot of work and involved planning months in advance, I was very proud of the work and was pleased with how the event turned out.
